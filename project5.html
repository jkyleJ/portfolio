Oppenheimer movie trailer recreation based on social media

Role

  • Motion Graphic Designer

Project Type

  • Promotional

Tools Used

  • Adobe After Effects
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Audition

Team

  • Jeongwon Jin - Designer

Duration

  • Oct 2023 - Dec 2023

Recreate the 30-second short movie trailers under the assumption that they will be streamed on Netflix, based on a mobile platform such as Tictok, Instagram and YouTube shorts for the film The Oppenheimer

My production journey commenced with the meticulous arrangement of footage, meticulously aligning it according to the preconceived storyboard. Prioritizing the s progression of my trailer, I initiated the process by fine-tuning the audio elements, and strategically orchestrating their cadence to establish the desired tempo. This deliberate sequence was complemented by the strategic incorporation of sound effects, strategically employed to accentuate and underscore pivotal moments within the trailer, thus imbuing each phase with heightened impact and resonance. Moving forward, the subsequent step involved the creation of dynamic motion graphics integrating typography elements. Initially crafted using Adobe Illustrator, the typography underwent a meticulous design process before being seamlessly imported into Adobe After Effects. .

Within this realm, I orchestrated the convergence of typographic artistry with motion graphic design, utilizing the latter platform to infuse life and movement into the typographic elements, thus enhancing their visual impact and engagement. This meticulous fusion of creative processes across software platforms culminated in the production of compelling and animated typographic compositions. Upon reaching the culmination of the stages, the transition into Adobe After Effects marked a pivotal phase. Here, my focal point revolved around the intricacies of harmonizing and unifying the pace, ensuring a seamless interplay between the meticulously curated visual elements and the bespoke audio components. This process involved a deliberate and detailed effort to synchronize and align the rhythm and timing, a crucial endeavour aimed at crafting a cohesive and immersive experience for the audience.

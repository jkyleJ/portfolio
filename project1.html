Oppenheimer Motion Graphics Promotional Package

Role

  • Motion Graphic Designer

Project Type

  • Promotional

Tools Used

  • Adobe After Effects
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Substance 3d Stager

Team

  • Jeongwon Jin - Designer

Duration

  • Jan 2024 - Mar 2024

Create a motion poster under the assumption that it will be streamed on Netflix, based on a digital display in a public place such as Dundas Square in Toronto or Time Square in New York.

This project commenced with the meticulous arrangement of footage. Prioritizing the seamless progression of my motion poster, I strategically orchestrated the objects to establish the desired tempo. This deliberate sequence was complemented by the strategic incorporation of the overall tempo, strategically employed to accentuate and underscore pivotal moments within the motion poster, thus imbuing each phase with heightened impact and resonance. The subsequent step involved the creation of dynamic motion graphics integrating typography elements with a 3D effect. Initially crafted using Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Substance 3D Stager, the typography underwent a meticulous design process before being seamlessly imported into Adobe After Effects.

Within this realm, I orchestrated the convergence of typographic artistry with motion graphic design, enhancing their visual impact and engagement. This meticulous fusion of creative processes across software platforms culminated in the production of compelling and animated typographic compositions. Upon reaching the culmination of the stages, the transition into Adobe After Effects marked a pivotal phase. Here, my focal point revolved around the intricacies of harmonizing and unifying the pace, ensuring a seamless interplay between the meticulously curated visual elements. This process involved a deliberate and detailed effort to synchronize and align the rhythm and timing, a crucial endeavour aimed at crafting a cohesive and immersive experience for the audience.
